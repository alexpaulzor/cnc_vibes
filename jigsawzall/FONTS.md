# Font evaluation for the letter-split puzzle

Generated by `font_eval.py`. Fonts are scored on how well they suit the puzzle's letter-splitting rule: every glyph should expose at least one **full-height thick vertical stroke (stem)** that a seam can bisect (detected by `glyph_origins.glyph_stroke_anchors`), and the face should be **loose enough** that neighbouring letters can be spaced a tab apart (side-bearing space vs. cap height). Only locally-installed fonts were tested; nothing was fetched online.

## Metrics

- **stem_coverage** - fraction of A-Z0-9 with >=1 splittable full-height stem (higher = better; 1.00 ideal).
- **avg_stems** - mean full-height stems per glyph (more = more choices of where to place a seam).
- **stem_thick** - dominant stem width as a fraction of glyph height (thicker = a more forgiving bisection target).
- **side_space** - mean advance-minus-ink width as a fraction of cap height (higher = looser = easier to keep a tab between letters; condensed faces score low here).
- **score** = 0.55*coverage + 0.25*min(side_space/0.30,1) + 0.20*min(avg_stems/2,1).

## Research needed (not installed, not fetched)

Fonts that would likely help the puzzle but are not installed locally (listed only - not downloaded):
- **DejaVu Sans Bold** - the pipeline's Linux fallback; very even, thick stems, generous spacing; good cross-platform baseline.
- **Roboto Bold / Roboto Slab Bold** - open, wide, thick uniform stems; slab variant adds vertical mass to A/V/W/X/Y.
- **Source Sans / IBM Plex Sans Bold** - open apertures, clean stems, comfortable spacing.
- **Archivo / Libre Franklin Bold** - grotesques with strong even stems and loose default tracking.
